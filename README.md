# AI-Assisted External Benchmarking Workflow

## Objective
This project explores how AI can improve external market benchmarking and decision support for strategy and growth teams.

The goal is to create a lightweight workflow that gathers external inputs, summarizes key themes, highlights relevant signals, and turns them into a more structured weekly or monthly digest for decision-making.

## Why I Built This
In strategy and business planning work, external benchmarking is often manual, fragmented, and time-consuming. I wanted to test how AI tools could improve the speed and quality of research, synthesis, and reporting.

This project reflects my interest in the practical application of AI in business workflows, especially in market intelligence, strategy support, and execution planning.

## What This Workflow Does
- collects external inputs such as articles, announcements, or market updates
- summarizes key points using AI tools
- groups findings by company, market, product, or theme
- highlights potential implications for business teams
- outputs a simple digest or structured summary

## Tools Used
- ChatGPT / Claude
- Python (optional, depending on workflow version)
- Markdown / Google Sheets / CSV
- Manual research inputs and workflow design

## Example Use Cases
- weekly competitor benchmarking
- monthly market trend summary
- product and pricing scan
- campaign and offer monitoring
- strategic decision support for leadership teams

## Current Scope
This is an experimental portfolio project focused on workflow design and practical business application rather than enterprise-grade engineering.

## Possible Next Steps
- connect to APIs or automated input sources
- classify signals by theme or strategic relevance
- create a simple dashboard layer
- compare output quality across different prompting approaches
- measure time saved versus manual benchmarking

## What I’m Learning
- where AI improves research and synthesis meaningfully
- where human judgment is still essential
- how to structure repeatable workflows instead of one-off prompts
- how to translate AI experimentation into measurable business value
